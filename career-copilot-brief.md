# Career Co-Pilot — Founding Brief

*A working strategy + build brief. Living document — update as you learn.*
Last updated: 2026-07-03

---

## 1. One line

An AI mentor that knows you deeply, tells you the truth, gives you your next concrete move, and can actually open a door — entered through the acute pain of a job search, retained as the operating system for your whole career.

---

## 2. The problem (and who has it)

People drift into work they don't like and get **stuck at forks in the fog** — the core pain isn't "I need a resume," it's **"I don't know my next move."** That moment is:

- **Acute** — it hurts most during a job search, a layoff, a "am I in the wrong career" crisis.
- **Underserved** — job boards assume you already know what you want; ChatGPT gives generic advice; human coaches cost $200–300/hr and you see them twice.
- **Navigational, not motivational** — users need a *map and compass*, not a nag or a cheerleader.

---

## 3. The wedge

> **"What's my next move — and why?"**
> One specific, personalized, contextual recommendation. The answer might be a job, a skill, a conversation, or a direction. **A move, not a list of listings.**

The job search is the *acute* flavor that gets people in the door. The navigation is the durable value that keeps them.

**Enter through the painkiller, retain through the vitamin:** they arrive bleeding ("I need out of this job"), you solve *that*, and in doing so you've earned the relationship that continues — "you got the job; is this the right rung, or a better-paid version of the same trap?"

---

## 4. Why this survives ChatGPT

Not "we're smarter than the model" — you *rent* the model, you'd lose that fight. You win on the stuff that lives **outside the model**:

- **OpenAI won't verticalize into job search** — low-margin, integration-heavy, trust-heavy, beneath a company selling general intelligence.
- ChatGPT is a **passive tool you drive turn-by-turn**, starting cold every time. This is an **agent that works while you're not looking, on a surface ChatGPT will never touch.**

---

## 5. The moat (all three compound over time; none are the UI)

1. **Compounding profile** — a longitudinal model of you that gets dramatically better by session 20. A cold model gives generic next moves; a model that's known you for months says *"you keep avoiding management across three jobs but keep applying to manager roles for the money — your next move is the staff-engineer track."* Value and moat are the same asset.
2. **The human network (doors)** — the one thing an AI mentor structurally *cannot* do alone: a real introduction/referral. This is why "community" belongs in the product — not as a forum, but as the door-opening function of mentorship.
3. **Earned trust to tell hard truths** — the default failure mode of every AI companion is sycophancy. An agent with the spine to disagree is the hardest *product* problem here and a real differentiator. A yes-man is a horoscope.

⚠️ **The voice interface is NOT the moat.** Voice agents are a commodity (Retell/Vapi/ElevenLabs/OpenAI Realtime). Voice is the *extraction mechanism* that feeds the moat — love it for that, don't mistake it for defensibility.

---

## 6. Product architecture (experience layer)

**Voice + chat in tandem — each has a job:**

| | Voice is for | Chat/text is for |
|---|---|---|
| Job | *Input & relationship* | *Output & reference* |
| Use | Discovery interview, hard-truth conversation, reflection, rambling that reveals you | The artifacts (tailored resume), the next-move tracker, async nudges, referral threads — anything to skim, save, act on |

**The core loop:**
> Voice deepens the profile → profile powers the chat artifacts → an artifact surfaces a gap → the next voice conversation explores that gap.

**The proof-of-thesis moment = the handoff.** Finish the voice intake → the resume agent opens *already knowing everything*, no re-explaining. ChatGPT can't (starts cold); Teal/Rezi can't (never talked to you). The handoff is your wow, your moat, and your demo at once.

**"Departments" are backend, not frontend.** Mentor / resume / jobs-filter / apply are internal specialists. To the user it must feel like **one coach who brings in specialists** — never five subscriptions to manage. Don't leak the org chart into the experience.

**Design principle — visible work, human at the forks.** The agent does the toil in the background but *shows* what it's doing and asks the user to steer at meaningful decisions. Autonomous on toil, human-in-the-loop on judgment. Blind trust is a bug.

## 7. Monetization — price the way value actually arrives

Career value is **spiky** (huge during a search, ~zero in the flat stretches). A flat $20/mo onto spiky value is a shape mismatch → churn. Don't fight the episodic nature; design for it. **Money must never gate the relationship.**

| Layer | What | Price | Why it protects the relationship |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Always-on relationship** | Mentor, context, ambient opportunity radar, "career OS" | **Free** (cheap to serve) | The moat + funnel. Never gated. |
| **Job-search mode** | Flip ON when actively searching: tailoring, filtering, tracking, prep, doors | **~$30–50/mo while active**, cancel anytime | Pay a premium only when it's on fire; nothing in the calm; **context persists** across the gap |
| **Upskilling recs** | Honest course/material recs in the calm | **Affiliate commission** | Monetizes flat periods *without charging the user*; funds the free tier |

**Rules:**
- The profile is **shared and always-on regardless of tier.** Paywall the context and you kill the golden goose.
- **Don't rush to monetize auto-apply** — charging per-apply incentivizes volume, the exact behavior that hurts users (see §9).
- Affiliate recs must be honest *even when there's no commission*, or trust dies.
- **"Free" has a bill** — an always-on voice mentor isn't free to run. Free tier = mostly async/text + low-frequency check-ins; expensive voice lives in the paid sprint. Design the free tier's *cost*, not just its price.

**Horizon: B2B2C.** Individuals are broke and churny — a structural ceiling. Durable money is paid by employers/bootcamps/universities/outplacement (companies already pay Randstad/LHH thousands per laid-off employee). Start B2C to prove the magic and build the data moat; keep B2B2C in peripheral vision.

## 8. Segmentation — pick a beachhead, they're opposite products

| | Late professionals ✅ | Colleges ⚠️ |
|---|---|---|
| Context to compound | **Deep** (moat strongest) | **Thin** (moat weakest — no track record) |
| Willingness to pay | **High** (hundreds for one good move) | Low individually |
| Sale | Individual, fast | Procurement, 6–18 mo (startup-killer) |
| Engagement | Self-motivated | Mandated → ignored (Handshake problem) |
| Loyalty | Clean (agent serves user) | Conflicted (school wants placement stats) |
| Trap | Ego/skepticism, "an AI mentors *me*?", harder to reach | Seat-count seduction: 1,000 mandated logins < 100 engaged profiles |

**Recommendation: beachhead = experienced professionals / career-switchers** — where your specific magic (deep context + hard truths + high-value next move) is strongest and the money is realest. **Colleges are a channel you earn later, not a starting point.** If you do go institutional, target **bootcamps, not universities** (faster sale, placement *is* their business, engaged adult switchers with real prior context).

**Anchor the final call on distribution:** which segment can *you* reach next week? Founder access can override the market math. *(Open question — see §12.)*

## 9. Riskiest assumptions (ranked) + cheapest test

1. **Will people talk to the agent honestly, and does the conversation extract something real?** → *If it only surfaces what's already on their LinkedIn, you have nothing.* **Test: Wizard-of-Oz, this week.**
2. **Does the handoff feel like magic?** → Have them do the same task in ChatGPT and compare blind. If it's not visibly better, the thesis is broken.
3. **Does the profile compound?** → Is session 5 noticeably better *because* of accumulated context? If it feels like session 1, no moat.
4. **Do they come back after landing a job?** → the vitamin/retention bet.
5. **Will the agent tell hard truths without becoming a yes-man?** → a product/prompt-design risk.

**The landmine (not an assumption — a known trap): auto-apply.** The bottleneck was never *volume* of applications. Blasting 500 makes outcomes *worse* (spam signal, torched reputation). Automate the **toil** (finding fitting opportunities, drafting, tracking, follow-up nudges); never automate the **judgment** (which jobs, what to say, hitting send on your name).

## 10. Phased build plan

| Phase | What you build | Goal | Time |
|---|---|---|---|
| **v0 — Wizard-of-Oz** | *You* are the agent. Interview 10 people by hand, hand-craft "next move + tailored resume." Zero code. | Truth on assumptions #1–2 | **This week** |
| **v0.5 — duct-tape core loop** | Voice intake → persistent profile → context-aware **handoff** to one artifact. Nothing else. Glue managed services, roll your own nothing. | Prove the handoff wow + compounding | **2–4 weeks solo** |
| **v1 — real users** | Auth, payments/tiers, job-board integrations, background "works-while-you-sleep" agent, first door/referral | First retained users | **Months** |

**Do Wizard-of-Oz first, no matter how tempting the code is.** If the hand-crafted handoff doesn't make people go "whoa," no stack fixes that.

**Explicitly NOT phase 1:** community/forum, apply-automation, affiliate courses, B2B2C sales, the multi-agent "departments" UI. Every one only works *because* the profile exists — build the profile-powered handoff first.

## 11. System design

### Shipping stack (v0.5 — duct-tape, managed services)

```
                        ┌─────────────────────────────┐
   User (browser) ─────▶│  Next.js app (v0.dev shell) │
     voice + chat       │  chat UI · artifact viewer  │
                        └───────────┬─────────────────┘
                                    │
              ┌─────────────────────┼───────────────────────┐
              ▼                     ▼                        ▼
   ┌──────────────────┐  ┌────────────────────┐  ┌────────────────────┐
   │  Voice layer     │  │  Agent orchestrator │  │  Profile store     │
   │  Vapi / Retell / │  │  (LLM: Claude)      │  │  Supabase/Postgres │
   │  OpenAI Realtime │─▶│  mentor · resume ·  │─▶│  structured profile│
   │  STT · TTS       │  │  jobs · handoff     │  │  + raw transcripts │
   └──────────────────┘  └─────────┬──────────┘  │  + embeddings      │
                                   │              └────────────────────┘
                                   ▼
                        ┌────────────────────┐
                        │  Artifact gen      │
                        │  resume / next-move│
                        └────────────────────┘
   Later: Stripe (billing) · job-board APIs · queue/cron (async agent) · referral graph
```

**Golden rule for v0.5:** duct-tape mode. Don't build your own vector store, auth, or voice pipeline. Ship the loop, learn the truth.

### The real system-design surface (target architecture — and your curriculum)

Each of these is a genuine senior-level system-design topic that *building this forces you to learn* — better than grinding problems in the abstract:

- **Real-time voice streaming** — latency budgets, barge-in/interruption handling, streaming STT→LLM→TTS, session state.
- **Persistent memory / context modeling** — the hard one. How do you store an *evolving* profile? Structured facts vs. raw transcripts vs. embeddings; retrieval strategy; how the profile updates without contradicting itself; summarization vs. full history. This is the moat, architecturally.
- **Multi-agent orchestration** — the mentor→resume→jobs **handoff** is a system-design problem: shared state, agent boundaries, how context passes between specialists, routing.
- **Background / async agents** — "works while you sleep" = job queues, cron/scheduled workers, webhooks, idempotency, retries.
- **Third-party integrations** — job boards / ATS: messy, rate-limited, unreliable real-world APIs; caching; failure handling.
- **Billing & entitlements** — Stripe, metered/sprint pricing, tier gating that *doesn't* gate the shared profile.
- **Scaling stateful conversations** — session affinity, cost control, per-user compute budgets (why free tier ≠ unlimited voice).

### Learning-mode vs shipping-mode — keep them separate

They optimize for **opposite things.** Shipping wants duct-tape and speed; learning wants you to build each layer and understand it. Doing both at once = over-engineering + shipping nothing.

- **v0.5 = shipping mode.** Managed services, roll your own nothing. Goal: truth.
- **Post-signal hardening = learning mode.** Deliberately re-architect the parts that matter (start with memory/context — it's the moat *and* the richest lesson) under real constraints.
- **Dogfood the loop:** you're user #1. The product says "your next skill gap is system design"; you learn it by hardening the very system you're building. Founder-market fit: you are your own archetypal user.

## 13. Session 2 refinements (2026-07-04)

### Positioning / mission
- **One line: "AI to help you — while the other AI replaces you."** The worker's ally in an unfair fight.
- Emotional core: scared, time-poor people **settle for less** just to start a paycheck. We compress time/effort/anxiety so they **don't settle** — without exploding the time curve.
- **North star = outcome quality** (did you land better than you'd have settled for), NOT applications sent. The opposite of the auto-apply spam trap.

### The stitched experience (v0 shape)
Philosophy: stitch the whole loop first, then progressively improve the value-generating parts. Valid *here* because the moat is the connective tissue (shared context), not any single tool. **Non-negotiable from day one: the shared understanding-map spine — every tool reads from and writes to it.**

| Stage | User does | Feeds the spine | Value type |
|---|---|---|---|
| **0 · Seed** | Drop resume → extract fields → editable template + pro-image tool | Structured history | Early win / acquisition (buildable now) |
| **1 · Mentor** | Voice call deepens aspirations, energizers, values, the gap | Inferred layer (moat context) | Moat-building (WoZ-able) |
| **2 · Engines** | Resume alignment (N aspiration variants) + opportunity filtering + prepared applications | Approve/reject = revealed prefs refine the map | **Durable value — the core** |
| **3 · Publish** | Photos/socials → generated site → domain or hosted | (reads the spine) | Early win + recurring revenue |

Effort ∝ inverse of defensibility: cheap on 0 & 3 (commodity), most effort on the Stage 2 loop.

### Key structural decisions
- **Resume = a set of aspiration-aligned variants** projected from the profile, not one static doc. Each opportunity → best-fit variant.
- **Auto-apply = auto-prepare → one-tap approve.** Agent queues ready-to-send tailored applications; human approves. NEVER autonomous blast. (Optional trusted auto-send toggle for power users later.)
- **Retention loop = "come back to see what changed."** Pull > push. Any push notification clears a brutal quality gate.

### Open problem (sharpest unsolved)
**Cold-start — "what changes in week one"**: before opportunity flow exists, the loop must still show real, visible progress (sharper resume variant *with a reason*, a named skill gap, a prep tip, one hand-sourced lead). Solve explicitly.

### Biggest gap in the whole plan
**Distribution.** The product is designed exhaustively; acquisition is barely touched. Red-ocean category, commodity at the point of entry. This is the next strategic work *after* the WoZ test.

## 14. Wizard-of-Oz run-sheet — do this week, no code

**Goal:** find out if the *magic* is real — does the conversation extract something non-obvious, and does the hand-crafted artifact beat what they'd get from ChatGPT?

**Setup**
- 5–10 people mid-search or recently job-hunting (ideally *not* close friends who'll just be nice).
- 30–40 min call. You are the mentor. Record with consent, or take fast notes. Get their resume beforehand if you can.

**The conversation (fill the map — don't interrogate)**
1. **Warm open:** "Where are you in your search, and how's it feeling?" *(emotional state + urgency)*
2. **Story probe:** "Tell me about the last thing at work you were genuinely proud of — or lost track of time doing." Follow the thread. *(skills + energizers)*
3. **Drainer:** "What drained you? What would make you quit tomorrow?" *(values, anti-preferences)*
4. **Ladder the goal:** "What are you looking for next?" → "why does that matter?" ×3 *(the real driver)*
5. **Gap check (the hard-truth test):** reflect a contradiction if you spot one — "You say you want to lead, but every story that lit you up was hands-on building. What do you make of that?"
6. **Close:** "If this worked perfectly, what would've changed in a month?"

**Then deliver (the handoff test)**
- Hand-craft: 1 tailored resume angle/variant + "here are 2–3 opportunities/directions worth your energy, *why* each fits the actual you, and your single next move for each."
- Have them *also* paste their resume + a JD into ChatGPT and compare **blind**.

**What to watch for (the real signals)**
- ✅ Did the conversation surface something NOT already on their resume/LinkedIn? *(If no → the moat is fake.)*
- ✅ Did your output visibly beat ChatGPT's? *(If no → no reason to exist.)*
- ✅ Did they lean in / get emotional / say "yeah, exactly"? *(The "someone gets me" signal.)*
- ✅ A week later: did they come back, act on it, or ask what's next? *(Retention signal.)*

**Kill criteria (be honest):** if 3+ people give shallow LinkedIn-tier answers *and* the output ties with ChatGPT, the thesis needs rework before you write a line of code.
